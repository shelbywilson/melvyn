17th-century writers in Latin

Charles du Fresne, sieur du Cange (French: [ʃaʁl dy fʁɛn sjœʁ dy kɑ̃ʒ]; December 18, 1610 in Amiens – October 23, 1688 in Paris, aged 77), also known simply as Charles Dufresne, was a distinguished French philologist and historian of the Middle Ages and Byzantium. == Life == Educated by Jesuits, du Cange studied law and practiced for several years before assuming the office of Treasurer of France.
